The Eiffel Tower, a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France, is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built it. The tower was constructed between 1887 and 1889 as the entrance arch to the 1889 World's Fair, which was held to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution.
